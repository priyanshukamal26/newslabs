# NewsLabs: The Simple, Complete Guide

Welcome to NewsLabs! If you're wondering what this platform is all about, you are in the right place. We are going to explain exactly what NewsLabs is, what it does, and how it works—in plain, simple English.

---

## 1. What is NewsLabs?

Imagine having a super-smart assistant who reads thousands of news articles on the internet every day. This assistant throws away the junk, figures out the real topic of each article, checks if the source is trustworthy, and then writes you a perfect summary of the most important news. 

That is exactly what NewsLabs is. It is a personalized, AI-powered news platform that organizes the world's news specifically for you.

## 2. How Does It Get the News?

News websites (like BBC, TechCrunch, ESPN) broadcast a signal called an **RSS Feed** when they publish a new story. 

NewsLabs taps directly into these feeds. You can pick and choose which websites you want NewsLabs to listen to. Instead of making you wait while it downloads every single article, NewsLabs is built for speed: it grabs articles in small, fast chunks and instantly pops them onto your screen the second they arrive.

## 3. The "AI Brain": Reading and Sorting

When a new article comes in, NewsLabs doesn't just show you the headline. It runs the article through its own internal "Brain" (we call this the NLP Engine). 

Here is what the Brain does to every single article:
1. **Finds the Topic**: It reads the text and mathematically figures out if the article is about *Technology, Politics, Sports, Space*, etc. It even has special rules to understand local news, like knowing that "Sensex" means Indian Business.
2. **Checks the Mood (Sentiment)**: It counts the positive and negative words to tell you if the news is generally uplifting, negative, or totally neutral.
3. **Opinion vs. Fact**: It spots phrases like "I think" or "We must" to warn you if an article is just someone's opinion, or if it is hard, factual reporting based on quotes and numbers.
4. **Reliability Score**: It grades the article on a scale of 0 to 100. If the title is clickbait ("You won't believe what happened next!"), the score drops. If it's from a trusted source and includes real data, the score goes up.

## 4. The Summarizer: Your Custom Reading Experience

Let's say you see an interesting headline, but you don't have time to read a massive 2,000-word article. 

When you click on the article, NewsLabs connects to massive Artificial Intelligence models (like the ones built by Google or Groq). It asks the AI to read the whole article and write you a summary on the spot.

The best part? You control how the AI talks to you. You can set it to:
- **Concise**: Give me the facts in 3 sentences, no fluff.
- **Balanced**: Give me a paragraph and 5 bullet points with key insights.
- **Detailed**: I really care about this topic; give me a deep, long summary explaining the exact details and why it matters.

## 5. Your Daily "Brief" (Automated Delivery)

You don't even have to open the NewsLabs website to get your news.

NewsLabs features an automatic scheduler. You can connect it to your Telegram or Discord app. You tell NewsLabs what times you want your news (Morning, Noon, Evening, or Night). 

When the time comes, NewsLabs will look at the articles from the last 24 hours, find the ones that are highly reliable and match the topics you care about, and send a beautiful, formatted message directly to your phone. It's like a personalized morning newspaper, delivered instantly.

## 6. Keeping Track of Your Habits

Finally, NewsLabs makes reading fun by keeping track of what you do:
- **Your Library**: You can "Save" articles to read later, or "Like" them to keep a permanent bookmark.
- **Your Stats**: It tracks how many articles you read, estimates how much total time you've spent reading, and tracks your "Login Streak" (how many days in a row you've read the news).

---

### In Summary
NewsLabs grabs the raw news from the internet, uses local math to categorize and grade it, uses giant AI models to summarize it perfectly for your reading style, and can deliver it directly to your phone on a schedule. It is your ultimate, personalized news room.
